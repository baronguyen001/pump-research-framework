# Entry and Exit

## Entry gate

Before opening a position, all checks should be written down:

- Total score is at least 70 or the strategy-specific reason is explicit.
- Tokenomics and the next unlock dates are understood.
- Liquidity and slippage are acceptable for the planned size.
- Position size is fixed before the trade.
- Exit plan and invalidation are written before entry.

## Scale-in ladder

| Tranche | Size | Confirmation |
|---:|---:|---|
| 1 | 30 percent | score first clears the gate |
| 2 | 30 percent | price or volume confirms |
| 3 | 25 percent | catalyst becomes official |
| 4 | 15 percent | breakout confirms and no red flag appears |

Never enter the full planned size at once just because the timeline is loud.

## Take-profit ladder

| Multiple | Sell fraction | Reason |
|---:|---:|---|
| 2x | 25 percent | recover risk and reduce pressure |
| 5x | 25 percent | lock additional profit |
| 10x | 25 percent | make the remaining position easier to hold |
| runner | remaining 25 percent | trail only while thesis remains valid |

## Time stop

For micro-caps and memecoins, a tight stop can be worse than no trade because
wicks are violent. Use a time stop instead: if the expected catalyst or social
reflexivity does not appear inside the written window, reduce or exit.

## Invalidation

Write one sentence before entry:

"I am wrong if..."

Examples:

- unlock pressure is larger than expected;
- liquidity leaves;
- smart-wallet accumulation reverses;
- the catalyst is cancelled;
- the narrative rotates to another category.
