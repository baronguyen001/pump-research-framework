# Pattern C: Airdrop and Launch

SURVIVORSHIP-BIAS CAVEAT: successful airdrops are visible. Failed farms,
sybil-filtered wallets, and post-TGE dumps are less visible.

## JUP

- Category: Solana aggregator.
- Approximate event: January 2024 airdrop.
- Public trigger: broad user distribution and Solana revival.
- Layers that helped: catalyst and on-chain usage.
- Layers that did not help enough: farming return depended on prior behavior.

## ENA

- Category: synthetic dollar and yield.
- Approximate event: 2024 airdrop and listing.
- Public trigger: points campaign, revenue story, exchange access.
- Layers that helped: narrative, catalyst, and protocol metrics.
- Layers that did not help enough: token incentive reflexivity can reverse.

## HYPE

- Category: perpetual DEX and L1.
- Approximate event: late 2024 airdrop.
- Public trigger: real exchange usage, trader distribution, strong product
  attention.
- Layers that helped: on-chain and catalyst.
- Layers that did not help enough: future valuation still depends on durable
  volume and competition.

## JTO

- Category: Solana liquid staking and MEV.
- Approximate event: December 2023 airdrop.
- Public trigger: Solana revival and exchange listings.
- Layers that helped: catalyst and ecosystem narrative.
- Layers that did not help enough: launch-day volatility made sizing hard.

## Dump counter-cases

LayerZero and EigenLayer showed how airdrop attention can become sell pressure
when recipients treat the token as compensation rather than ownership. Airdrops
need post-TGE demand, not just pre-TGE farming.

## Pattern summary

The best airdrop launches combine real usage, clear revenue or fees, broad
distribution, and a reason for holders to stay after the claim.
