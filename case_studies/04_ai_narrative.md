# Pattern D: AI Narrative

SURVIVORSHIP-BIAS CAVEAT: AI tokens are especially vulnerable to story-first
valuations. Many had strong narratives before durable usage existed.

## VIRTUAL

- Category: AI-agent launchpad and ecosystem.
- Approximate window: late 2024.
- Approximate multiple: very large early move.
- Public trigger: agent launchpad narrative and viral agent experiments.
- Layers that helped: narrative, social, and catalyst.
- Layers that did not help enough: fundamentals were hard to value in real time.

## AI16Z

- Category: AI-agent DAO and open-source framework ecosystem.
- Approximate window: late 2024.
- Approximate multiple: large move into billion-dollar valuation territory.
- Public trigger: open-source agent tooling, social reflexivity, category
  momentum.
- Layers that helped: narrative and social.
- Layers that did not help enough: valuation outran measurable user value.

## FET, RNDR, and AGIX-style AI beta

- Category: broader AI infrastructure and compute narrative.
- Approximate window: 2024.
- Public trigger: AI market attention and alliance or infrastructure stories.
- Layers that helped: narrative and catalyst.
- Layers that did not help enough: beta exposure cut both ways.

## 2025 AI-agent drawdown

Public AI-agent baskets suffered large drawdowns after the first reflexive wave.
The lesson is not that the narrative was fake. The lesson is that narrative
speed can exceed product maturity, and exits matter.

## Pattern summary

AI narratives need stricter reality checks: working users, fee capture, developer
activity, and whether the token has a role beyond attention.
