# Layer 2: Social

Purpose: distinguish early organic attention from late paid promotion.

The sweet spot is not silence and not mass media. It is the middle: respected
mid-tier accounts discuss a token or category repeatedly, but the wider market
has not converted it into a crowded trade.

| Public tool | Use |
|---|---|
| LunarCrush | mentions, sentiment, and trend change |
| Kaito public views | mindshare and account clusters |
| DexScreener | trending pairs and social links |
| Galxe and Layer3 | quest activity and community tasks |
| Public X search | manual account quality review |

Strong signals worth 20 or more points:

- Mid-tier accounts mention the asset organically several times in 24 hours.
- Mention rate rises more than 200 percent week over week from a low baseline.
- Social attention lines up with the narrative layer instead of appearing alone.
- Community activity has substance: docs, demos, governance, or working users.

Weak or red-flag signals:

- Most mentions come from accounts with tiny followings and identical wording.
- Sentiment rises while price and liquidity weaken.
- Paid-promo accounts drive the timeline.
- Tier-1 influencers and mass-market outlets are already everywhere.

How to wire this layer to your own data: track ticker mentions, account quality,
baseline change, and whether links point to real product activity. A simple CSV
updated by hand is enough for this repo.
