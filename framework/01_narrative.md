# Layer 1: Narrative

Purpose: identify where capital and attention are rotating before the move is
obvious to mainstream media.

| Public tool | Use |
|---|---|
| CoinGecko categories | 7d and 30d category returns |
| DefiLlama | TVL by chain, protocol, and sector |
| Artemis | active users, fees, and chain activity |
| Token Terminal | protocol revenue and valuation context |
| Kaito public views | narrative mindshare and leading accounts |
| Polymarket | event markets that reveal early attention |

Strong signals worth 20 or more points:

- A category rises more than 50 percent in a week while still being lightly
  covered by mainstream media.
- TVL, fees, active users, or stablecoin inflows rise with price, not after it.
- A public prediction market or sector report begins tracking the theme before
  retail channels are saturated.
- The narrative has a simple sentence: "modular execution," "stablecoin rails,"
  "AI-agent launchpad," or similar.

Weak or red-flag signals:

- The narrative is already the front-page topic everywhere.
- Only price is moving; usage, revenue, TVL, and developer activity are flat.
- The category name is vague enough to include anything.
- A few paid posts create the whole narrative.

How to wire this layer to your own data: build a daily table of category return,
TVL change, fee change, and public mindshare. Keep it source-linked. Do not score
a category highly just because a token chart already moved.
